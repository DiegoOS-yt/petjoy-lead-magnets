The Challenge

Most pet stores are flying blind

While big box retailers run predictive algorithms on billions of transactions, independent stores still rely on gut feel, vendor reps, and expired trade show data.

Overloaded with SKUs, underloaded with signal

The average independent store carries 2,400 SKUs but tracks performance on fewer than 200 of them. The other 2,200 are costing you margin every week.

Buying decisions made months too late

Trade publications report trends 60 to 90 days after they break. By the time you reorder a winner, the big chains already have 90-day stock of it on their shelves.

Cash locked in dead inventory

Our audit of 200 stores found an average of $3,192 per month trapped in products with sell-through rates below 40%. That money belongs in your best-performing categories.
