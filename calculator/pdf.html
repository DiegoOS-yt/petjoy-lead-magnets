The Real Problem

Your Inventory Is Quietly Draining Your Margins

Most independent pet store owners know their busiest products. What they don't know is how much the slow-movers are costing them. Dead inventory sits on shelves consuming capital, occupying space, and blocking better-performing products from taking their place.

This isn't a purchasing discipline problem. It's a data visibility problem. You can't optimize what you can't measure.

📦
Shelf Space Opportunity Cost

Every linear foot occupied by a 30-day-stale product is a foot that could display a high-velocity item generating 3x the turns.

💸
Cash Flow Compression

Dollars locked in unsold inventory are dollars unavailable for payroll, marketing, or purchasing the products customers actually want.

📉
Margin Erosion on Markdowns

When dead stock must be cleared, it typically sells at 20-40% below cost, compounding the original loss with a discount hit.

🔁
The Reorder Cycle Trap

Without visibility into velocity data, most stores re-order based on gut feel and sales rep recommendations, perpetuating the same mistakes every quarter.
