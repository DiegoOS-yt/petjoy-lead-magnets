Section 01

Brand Strategy

The foundational architecture that governs every decision PetJoy makes — from product selection to the language on an invoice.

Strategic Note

Most B2B pet product brands compete on price. PetJoy competes on curation certainty — the promise that every SKU has been pre-validated for sell-through. This is a defensible moat that price cuts cannot destroy.

Brand Archetype
The Curator
PetJoy doesn't sell products — it edits the market. Every SKU Michelle Joy selects goes through a 7-point curation framework before it reaches a subscriber's door. The Curator archetype builds trust through discernment, not volume.
Brand Energy
Confident Warmth
The authority of a category expert combined with the care of a trusted supplier who has personally tested every product. Never cold. Never corporate. Warm like a partner who genuinely wants your store to win.
Brand Style
Premium Simplicity
No clutter. No noise. Every touchpoint — from the unboxing experience to the monthly invoice — communicates that PetJoy respects the buyer's time and believes that simplicity is itself a luxury.
Mission Statement
"PetJoy exists to give independent pet store owners and veterinary clinics the curation intelligence that big-box retail pays millions to develop — delivered monthly, without the overhead."
Value 01
Curation Over Quantity
We evaluate 400+ SKUs per quarter and ship only the top 12. Every product earns its spot or it doesn't ship. "Enough selection to matter. Tight enough to trust."
Value 02
Independent First
Every feature, every pricing decision, every logistical choice is designed for the 38,000 independent operators — not the chains. If it works for PetSmart, it's not in the box.
Value 03
Radical Simplicity
Curated monthly. Ready to display. Zero research required on the buyer's end. The complexity exists inside PetJoy so it doesn't have to exist in your store.
Value 04
Joy Is the Product
The subscriber's unboxing experience is held to the same standard as the product inside. Every touchpoint — including the packing note — is considered. Joy is not a byproduct. It is the product.
The Brand Promise
If a product we curate doesn't achieve at least 80% sell-through in your store within 30 days, we replace it — free. No questions. No paperwork. That's what it means to stand behind curation.

    Section 05

    Voice & Tone Guide

    How PetJoy sounds in every context — from a LinkedIn post to an invoice email. Consistency here is what makes a brand feel premium.

    Warm
    Warm doesn't mean soft. It means treating the store owner as an intelligent partner, not a transaction. PetJoy speaks the way an experienced buyer friend would speak — candid, helpful, never condescending. The tone that says "I've already done the research so you don't have to."
    Sharp
    Sharp means one idea per sentence. Sharp means no filler words. Sharp means the store owner knows the point of the message within the first two lines. PetJoy never buries the lead. The data point comes before the explanation, always.
    Joyful
    Joy isn't frivolous — it's the brand's core value expressed in every interaction. The unboxing should feel like Christmas morning. The invoice email should not feel like a dentist appointment. There is always room for one line that makes a store owner smile.
    Tone Spectrum — Where PetJoy Lives
    Fully CasualFully Corporate
    PetJoy sits at 65% professional, 35% casual — expert enough to be trusted, human enough to be liked.
