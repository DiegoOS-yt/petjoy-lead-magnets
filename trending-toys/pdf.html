Editor's Note

The Data Behind the Rankings

Every quarter, PetJoy analyzes sell-through velocity across 200+ independent pet retailers in our network to surface the products gaining momentum — and flag those approaching stall. What you're holding is the result of 8 weeks of continuous tracking across 847 toy SKUs.

The Trending Score is not a manufacturer's sales figure or a trade show popularity contest. It's a composite metric built from three inputs: sell-through rate within 30 days, week-over-week velocity increase, and reorder frequency across our retailer network. A score of 90+ means a product is a category leader. A score below 60 is a warning flag.

Q2 2026 saw three clear trends emerge: enrichment toys moved from niche to mainstream, with interactive puzzle feeders claiming four of the top 10 slots. Natural material toys — specifically antler, rubber, and bamboo variants — outperformed synthetic alternatives across all price bands. And the $14.99–$22.99 price window proved to be the sweet spot, with 38 of the top 50 products living within that range.

Q2 Key Findings
Enrichment Dominates
Puzzle feeders and enrichment toys now represent 28% of all toy velocity data — up from 17% in Q2 2025. The category is no longer niche. Any store without 6+ enrichment SKUs is leaving dollars on the floor.
Price Band Clarity
The $14.99–$22.99 window accounts for 76% of the top 50. Products under $9.99 are declining as consumers invest more in quality. Products over $35 move well in stores with strong loyalty programs — slow everywhere else.
